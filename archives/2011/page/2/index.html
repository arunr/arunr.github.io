8 years at Microsoft: A tribute to my managers

I joined Microsoft 8 years ago to this day. Many of my friends from business school were surprised - I was a LAMP stack kinda guy who had spent more time on the command line than in the classroom! One of my friends told me, “You are too free-spirited to last more than a year in the corporate world”, and I had responded with, “I’ll check it out for a year or two, and then I’ll go do something else if I don’t like it!”. It surprises me how fast these 8 years have gone by - it is the passion for customers, the caring about the details &amp; the smarts of the people of Microsoft that first made me stay, and then fall in love with this company (or it may be the more mundane fact that I joined Microsoft on Valentine’s Day!) However, more than anything, I’ve had the most wonderful managers at Microsoft: people who have cared, people I have been able to learn from, and people I have hoped to emulate. This post is a tribute to the six great managers I’ve had during my 8 years at Microsoft (I know some of you guys are reading this - you always taught me to “ask for forgiveness, not permission”, so forgive me if I misrepresent you in any way!)

Learning to listen to music

Dear Nika, It makes me happy to see how much you like listening to music! Music is happiness, music is a balm, music is magic! When I was younger, I used to claim tastes and preferences in music that would give me identity - “I listen to sixties rock-and-roll,” I used to say, or “My favorite type of music is swamp rock”. This was a way to look cool, express my sense of self, be part of a group or worse, subtly exclude someone from a group - none of which had anything to do with music. I am older now, more comfortable with who I am, not having to be someone else for the sake of others. Now, at last, I finally can just listen to music, without any of my pride getting in the way!
