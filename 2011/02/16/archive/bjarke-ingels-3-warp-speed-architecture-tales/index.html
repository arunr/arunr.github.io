Bjarke Ingels: 3 warp-speed architecture tales

2011-02-16

Check out this talk called “3 warp-speed architecture tales“ by Bjarke Ingels.

The thing I really liked about this talk was the idea that was being explored that great design can win on multiple goals - sustainability, aesthetics, cost and so on. This is a constant struggle in any design process, on how to trade-off between things, and typically the discussion degrades to “A or B” kind of thinking very rapidly. I think in most cases, it is possible - through exploration and by developing a deep understanding across diverse fields of information and synthesizing this information - to come up with a design that achieves all goals, not just satisfactorily, but in a way that transcends any other alternate design.

The video that Bjarke plays in the end, “The Seven Peaks of Azerbaijan” was way, way cool. Bjarke Ingels has been called “architectures own Lady Gaga” by Metropolis Mag, so he one to be watched!
