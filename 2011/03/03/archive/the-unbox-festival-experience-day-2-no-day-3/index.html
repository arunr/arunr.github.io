The UnBox Festival Experience – Day #2

2011-03-03

Saturday at the UnBox Festival was great too! There were good sessions by John Thakara, Nico MacDonald, Harsh Purohit & Richard Eisermann in the morning. I found the talks by John & Harsh very interesting, and providing contrasting perspectives to the issues of sustainable development.

John spoke of the ethics of design (mentioning the terrific book Nudge along the way), a design oath, the core values of a restorative economy and how that would be based on an “unconditional respect for life”. He spoke of how reducing emissions was not enough - he said (loosely paraphrasing), “A judge doesn’t let of a thief with a sentence of ‘Steal less the next time’, so why should we ask for reduced emissions?”. He gave an example of how it takes 75kgs of material to actually make a 200 gm iPhone, which I found quite shocking! John also referred to a book I haven’t read called Small Change - it seems worth a read. John also referred to the Buckminister Fuller Challenge, which I hadn’t heard of (and that sounds absolutely fascinating!).

Harsh was a contrast to John. He runs a company called Cognito, which is a sustainability consulting company. Harsh spoke about how change happens top-down, how influencing business to do better business is what would lead to long term change, and the importance of not ignoring the financial aspects of our lives. He talked about how environmental, social & financial were all important aspects to consider, saying that sustainability was at the intersection of these 3 things. He also spoke about his 4 P’s of sustainability - Prosperity, Protest, Policies & Preservation. It was a great talk, with lots of references and videos, and I wish I could have a copy of it!

The afternoon saw a number of fantastic talks around the topic of “Documenting Culture”, by Parmesh Sahani, Apala Chavan, Rajesh Dahiya & Santosh Desai. The stand-out talk among these for me was the talk by Santosh Desai, which was funny, interesting and instructive all in one! Santosh talked about culture as soap - he had this fantastic quote by Krishna Kumar from “A memory of coming to life” - I couldn’t find the quote, but it was about culture as soap, and how it washes away the struggles of living and yet it is as though the soap is made of the rinse-water. He spoke of the “Infinite Knead” (!), and how culture is like kneading dough. He spoke about the word “Dhishoom!” in Hindi movies, and about cultural differences - how western movies have the punch, and Indian movies the slap!

The afternoon also saw 4 talks around future design interventions. I don’t remember the names of all speakers, but the one who stood out & stayed in my memory was Tithiya Sharma, who is doing the 100 Heroes Project. Tithiya spoke with passion & her stories of everyday, working-class heroes was humbling. Her message of “Create value, Make friends with your fear and If it itches, scratch” was powerful (and made me shameful for thinking about doing many great things which I never acted upon!).

Late afternoon, I attended a workshop on “Crafting Livable Cities”, led by Ajit Mohan from McKinsey & Madhav Raman of Anagram Architects. We had a great discussion, in which I believe I got a little excited & confrontational (!). I wish we had more time during the workshop - we barely got to scratch the surface of the topic, and most of the discussion ended up centering around low-cost housing, which I believe is only a small part of the “Crafting Livable Cities” theme. I did love the presentation by Madhav though - it traced the history of Delhi city, and showed maps of how the city has changed over the last 600 years, which I feel is a great way to look at urban planning.

Overall, Saturday was another fantastic day at the conference & I really enjoyed myself at UnBox. Due to sudden and unanticipated excitement at my friends place (where I happened to be staying over), I couldn’t make the workshops on Sunday - but it was OK, because the excitement was worth missing any workshop for!
