The UnBox Festival Experience - Day #1

2011-03-02

I went to the UnBox Festival last week (Thu - Sun) at New Delhi, and it was a fantastic design conference, one of the best I have been to. Kudos to the team that put the conference together - it was very well organized, and there was not a single hitch in the entire program. Over the 4 days, there were a number of interesting talks & ideas that came up, and I’m just going to capture some of the highlights in this post (most of these are from rough notes that I had taken down, so they may seem scatter-brained!).

Before I get started, there is one confession I must make - I love the street food in Delhi so much that each day of the conference I would eat the fancy lunch at the conference (Indonesian Rice, Polenta with Tomato Sauce, different types of Pasta and a choice of Tiramisu or Blueberry Cheese Cake) and then duck out to walk to my favorite Sardarji Ka Dhaba and have a second lunch of Chhole Kulche, Rajma Chawal or Mooli Parathas!

OK, so with that out of the way, let us get started, describing below the talks on Friday:

  • The first talk was by MP Ranjan. It was a semi auto-biographical talk that spanned many topics - design, sustainability, education etc. Some of my rough notes -

    • The idea of “My Square Foot” (leave the place below your feet better than when you occupied it).
    • The sustainability posters from the World Economic Forum / Davos 2009 (also here).
    • Discussion around design as a seed, design as politics and design as culture.
    • Point about 400 design schools in China in the next decade, with only 4 being opened in India.
    • A fantastic contribution to the creative commons - a book by MP Ranjan & his wife called “Handmade in India”, which can be downloaded from here.
    • Ranjan spoke about his blogs and about most of his material being available for anyone to download - very cool!

  • The next talks was by Lakshmi Murthy. Lakshmi spoke mainly about communication design, in the context of informing rural Indian children about puberty & sexual issues. It was an excellent talk, and the work she is doing deserves a lot of support & respect. She pointed us to her website, Vikalp Design, for more information around communication design. The one comment she made that stuck in my head - “This is the first design conference I am being invited to, usually I only get invited to development conferences”!

  • The third talk was by Ayush Chauhan, one of the organizers of the conference. I wasn’t impressed by his talk as much as the talks by the previous speakers, but the few things he talked about that stayed with me - the videos from the Potty Project that his company is working on which were funny/sad, and his slide where he talked about “Observation, Conversation & Interaction” as being core to good design.
  • The fourth talk of the day, before lunch, was by VK Madhavan. What a fantastic talk! I can’t really describe it, other than to say that it was one of the few talks that managed to move me, while being very much in the center - it wasn’t anti-market or pro-market, as much as being pro-development. The main thing that I took away from VK’s talk - Life is complex, and so integrated design is important - don’t oversimplify or be simplistic in design, that seems to be the trend right now. Think through governance, systems, business models, economics etc. when designing development solutions, not just one aspect. The one thing that he said, to paraphrase - “Measurement is very important, but sometimes once you measure, it is important to continue what you are doing and go on.” - the point being that while measurement and efficiency was important, sometimes you must continue onward after measuring. The other thing that VK talked about was about not having ego in design, and how this was very critical to making real progress. An excellent talk overall!

  • The four short talks in the afternoon were:

    • A talk by Ashish Rajpal from iDiscoveri around the work they have been doing with their XSeed curriculum. The few things from this talk that stuck - the recognition that teacher training wasn’t as sticky as focusing on lesson plans and engaging activity/experience based learning methods & the focus shift from teachers to students as the real customer.
    • The next talk was by Meena Kadri, from OpenIDEO. I didn’t enjoy the talk and didn’t really take away much from it - I think I have an issue with listening when people read out from a printed page when they are talking, and Meena seemed to be doing this.
    • The following talk was brilliant - the most funny, engaging talk of the 2 day conference, by Kapil Das of Blind Boys. The talk was irreverent and funny in a very under-stated way. It is hard to capture the spirit of the talk, except to say that I’d like to deliver a talk like that! He spoke of photography, reclaiming public spaces & his most brilliant last slide - “Just do it, I’m loving it, Impossible is nothing, Next is what”!.
    • There were 2 other talks - one by Ashutosh Phatak of Blue Frog and one by Pooja Sood from Khoj. I didn’t find either talk very memorable. The one link I took away from the talk by Pooja was for Periferry, a place for art practices based in Guwahati, Assam.
      Over all, it was a really good set of talks on Friday. In the afternoon, there was a work-shop on Design Jamming by Bram Pitoyo. I found the workshop itself OK, but the thing I enjoyed most was seeing how different people approach the same problem in different ways! (especially given that I am an a-type engineer!).
