Act2 - My new journey

2013-07-26

“All the world’s a stage, …

And one man in his time plays many parts”

(Shakespeare / As You Like It / Act 2 Scene 7)
“In the beginners mind there are many possibilities, in the experts mind there are few.” (Suzuki Shunryu)
Today is my last day at Microsoft. I completed 10 fantastic years at the company a few months ago, and now is the time for new beginnings. I was looking back on my last 10 years, and there were so many stories worth re-telling –

  • My first day at the company after graduating from business school when I got a bunch of PreFix bugs to fix (that brought me down to earth real quick – “What, I don’t get to do strategy?!”)
  • Revising a presentation multiple times till 2 am at night to get it just perfect, and then crashing in my manager’s hotel room, and waking up at 4:30 am to see him doing his yoga routine (you know who you are!)
  • Consoling a sobbing visitor from Redmond, who found all the food in Hyderabad too spicy, and so she ordered a yoghurt buttermilk – only to find pieces of green chilli & ginger in the drink!
  • Getting a card trick & a jigsaw puzzle to solve as interview questions when I moved to Redmond, and then getting to work on OfficeLive with some of the finest people I’ve known – you were like family!
  • Jogging from home in Kirkland to building 32 at 2 am at night to take care of a Live Site issue soon after OfficeLive launched (my car had broken down!)
  • Getting into a fight with my office mate – she came back from a vacation and cried out “Oh, my plants – they’re all dead – why didn’t you water them?”, and I replied “What plants?! I didn’t know we had plants in our office!”
  • Moving back to India – and among other features, working on the carousel – where we had daily arguments on the angle & speed of the carousel! Those who worked on it, you guys know remember this one.
  • Sitting quietly in my first meeting as GPM of Windows Azure Cache and being mistaken for a new college hire PM – I was asked to spec the disaster recovery feature! (I agreed J).
    Oh, I could go on endlessly… and while the memories are good, what made it special was the people, the individuals – genuine, caring and each one someone I could learn from.

My plan when I joined the company was to spend a year, and then go back to my start-up roots – I decided to defer that plan by a year, and then another, and then one more – only because I was learning daily, and the people were so great.

Anyway, enough rambling – what’s next, you ask? I don’t quite know yet: immediately, I am going to be volunteering at my daughter’s school (working with primary school children – I’m excited about that!), and I’m going to be working closely with a start-up in Delhi.

However, what I’m looking forward to is the blank canvas, the open road – I want to re-discover, revive, renew – see things with fresh eyes yet again, with a beginners mind. I’m going to be writing code (yes!), following my pretentions of being literary, teaching, travelling and exploring a few ideas.

Let us stay in touch – follow my blog at www.act2.me, and write to me at arunr@act2.me, and if you have a short attention span – then I’m on twitter too (appa).

“Afoot and light-hearted I take to the open road, Healthy, free, the world before me,

The long brown path before me leading wherever I choose.”

(Walt Whitman, Song of the Open Road)
“I will write you a letter from time to time,

As I’m ramblin’ you can travel with me too.

With my head, my heart and my hands, my love, I will send what I learn back home to you.

So it’s fare thee well my own true love,

We’ll meet another day, another time.” (Bob Dylan, Farewell)
