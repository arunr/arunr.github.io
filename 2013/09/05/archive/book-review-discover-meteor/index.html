Book Review: Discover Meteor

2013-09-05

[caption id=”” align=”alignright” width=”246”] Discover Meteor[/caption]

I read and worked through the examples of the Discover Meteor book yesterday (“The Main Sequence” edition), here’s a review of the book:

First, the 3 pros:

  • Quality of writing & pictures: Excellent. The concepts of Meteor are explained in simple language and with very easy to understand pictures. It is easy to get through the book very fast, because the writing is straight-forward and simple.
  • Quality of code & examples: Very good, easy to follow along, and all the code is correct and works first time.
  • Explanation of concepts: The sidebars that explain Meteor concepts are well written, especially the two sidebars on publications & subscriptions and denormalization.
    Now, the 2 cons:
  • The price: At $39, it is expensive for the 200-odd pages that the book packs. While useful, I would hesitate to pay this price. As a side note, I considered the other editions (Red Giant and White Dwarf) that I would have loved to get, but balked at the price, which is even more expensive in Indian Rupees (considering that the Rupee is now at 65 to the $).
  • Stops short: The book goes through the example application in detail, and covers some concepts in the sidebars. I would have liked it to cover an application end-to-end, go deeper into the concepts behind Meteor and also provides a API reference. This book stopped short of going deep into the details of Meteor, and I was left feeling a little thirsty for more information. My ideal book would be structured like “Agile Web Development with Rails“. Of course, areas like testing are also not covered in much depth, but this is understandable given that Meteor itself is evolving fast on these areas.
    Overall, I found that this is a great (if expensive) way to get started with Meteor, but it will not become the definitive guide on Meteor for me - which I was hoping it would be. Overall, I give it a 3.5 out of 5 star rating!

(I am going to be reading through the book leisurely again over the weekend, and I”ll post any additional review comments below this line!)
