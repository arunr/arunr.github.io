Teaching programming to homeschoolers

2013-08-07

I started to volunteer last week at a small school. Really, it is less a school and more a group of parents who homeschool their children.

I am currently working with one of the children who is interested in learning programming - and I have a ton of questions (further below) on how best to teach programming.

The first week of facilitating programming has been fun, the things we’ve worked on are:

  • Playing a “text-based adventure game” called the “Princess in the Pyramids”, where I play the computer and the child has to direct the computer with commands like “Forward”, “Swim”, “Fight” or “Talk”! :)
  • Learning to read, write and speak (dih-dih-dah-dah!) Morse Code.
  • Looking at Morse Code in detail - lets say a dot is a 0 and a dash is a 1- then how many dots and dashes will it take to represent the letters in the English language?
  • Trying to decode an article written in a secret language.
  • Playing Hangman - so as to understand the frequency of letters in the English language - useful for decoding that letter!
  • Playing my favourite game, Cows & Bulls to…. well, just to have fun!
    Next week, we’re thinking of moving on to Braille and understanding that system of coding information. We’re also going to build a simple telegraph machine: that should be fun, and should set the basis of moving from Morse code to actual transmission of information over a distance!

I would love to hear comments and ideas from readers on how best to teach programming to homeschoolers (btw, if you’re a grammar nut like me, check out this great post on the word homeschooler! :)).

The questions I have that I’m looking to get ideas on are:

  • How best to teach programming thinking without using a computer? (it’s a big distraction!)
  • How important is it to cover concepts like binary, boolean algebra, transistors and gates, addition & subtraction (twos complement etc.)?
  • When the day comes when we sit down in front of the computer - what are the right tools & techniques to introduce computing?
  • Does anyone know of a good reference to build a real working computer using Lego blocks - I’m not thinking MindStorms, but rather thinking something like the Babbage Engine :)
    Thanks, and do post your comments & ideas.
