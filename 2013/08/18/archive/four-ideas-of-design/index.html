Four ideas of design

2013-08-18

I gave a talk on “Four ideas of design” at the Indian School of Business as part of an all day workshop on Design Thinking & Innovation. I have run this workshop at the Indian School of Business for the last 2 years along with Abhimanyu Kulkarni & Koel Chatterjee (from Philips Design Lab).

The workshop is a lot of fun for the participants - and for us! As a participant, you go through a real design process - observing people, creating personas, thinking about the problem from different perspectives, writing scenarios, prototyping ideas (using play-doh, paints and other fun material), making a poster for the design and presenting it to the audience.

Here are the slides I use to kick-off the workshop:

The 4 ideas of design that I talk about are:

  • Design is business - it is a core part of business, and everyone in the company is responsible for it.
  • Design is a repeatable process - observe people, write scenarios, prototype, iterate
  • Design as conversation - like good conversation, it brings together people.
  • Design as lifelong learning - study the liberal arts, sketch, travel, be curious about people
    I pose a few questions to ponder over during the talk:
  • What outcome are you designing for? (and relative notions of good & bad)
  • What are you not going to do in your design? (related to your own principles)
  • When are you done? (is a design ever “done”?)
    Being self-taught in the design approach, I don’t have the formal knowledge that many students from design schools have. I see this as an advantage though - it has allowed me to form my own opinions on what I like, what is good & what isn’t and what works. It has given me a deep appreciation of the importance of design, how to participate in a design process and (as a product manager) when to suggest that we may be “done”!

I love doing this workshop - Abhimanyu & Koel facilitate this workshop wonderfully well, and bring their formal knowledge of the design process to this workshop; I try and bring in my product managers perspective, and offer my informal and learnt knowledge of design!

This years challenge was a lot of fun: the observation was that there are a lot of middle-aged, overworked executives who are overweight and have no time to exercise, the challenge was to come up with a design for a product, service or experience that would help these people work-out.

The ideas from the participants at the end of the workshop were great - and their play-doh models were a lot of fun! What I loved most though were the presentations from the participants - enthusiastic and humorous - an energizing end to a fun-filled (and hopefully educational) day of learning about design.
