What is program management?

2013-08-11

I was a Program Manager at Microsoft for 10 years - and over these years, I often got asked this question: “What is program management?” (or its variant - “What does a program manager do?”).

I heard this question from various people - at colleges when we went for hiring, from new program managers, from friends who were curious why I enjoyed my work so much, and at times from developers & testers who wondered whether we did anything at all!

I once told a friend that I was a program manager at Microsoft - and he said, “Oh, in which case you’ll become Windows Explorer when they promote you!” (here’s a picture of Program Manager from Windows 3.1!)

[caption id=”” align=”aligncenter” width=”512”] Program Manager[/caption]

I knew that I was more than a collection of icons (!), but how could I best describe the work that I did? There were many test-book definitions I heard when I was a new program manager, but they fell flat for me:

Over time, I developed my own definitions and narrative on what program management is & what a program manager does. These felt closer to reality to me, because they were they way that I viewed the role:

I was always loved about the program manager role - to me, a romantic, it felt like being a super-hero: the Clark Kent day job of project management, meetings and discussions of source-tree branching (!), and the Superman night job of defining (& refining, refining) the product, and dreaming of changing the world (even if just a little bit!).

You were bound to be misunderstood, and as a result, be a little neurotic! But the joy of a real, shipped product, and if you were lucky, a positive comment from just one real user - that joy was incomparable & was worth it all.

(Over the years at Microsoft, I wrote two essays on Program Management called “The Zen of Program Management” and “More Zen of Program Management”. I will be writing more about program management / product management on this blog - do follow me if you’re interested!).
