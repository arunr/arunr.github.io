The Fiddler & the Ghost

Once upon a time, long long ago, there was a fiddler. A fiddler is a person who plays a violin, which is also called a fiddle. The fiddler loved playing his fiddle, and when he picked up the fiddle and started playing, a peaceful, child-like smile spread across his face. The fiddler used to play his violin from morning till night, in summer and in winter, and even in the rain! He would play his fiddle sitting under the shade of a tree during the summer and in a temple during the monsoons or the winter. The fiddler played the violin really well, but he was very poor, for he used to love playing his fiddle so much that he didn’t even think for a minute of becoming rich or making money. “I have all the riches of the world in this wonderful fiddle,” he used to say, holding his old wooden violin lovingly. Then he would start to play his fiddle, and all the people nearby would stop what they were doing and would come and sit by him to hear him play. They would be enchanted by the music and they would clap or cry out in appreciation! They would then insist that he stay for lunch or dinner, and they would treat him well and sometimes even give him money.
