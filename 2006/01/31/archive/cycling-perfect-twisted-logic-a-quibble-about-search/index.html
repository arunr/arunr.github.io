Cycling, perfect twisted logic & a quibble about search

2006-01-31

I’ve been cycling once a week to work - I started about a month back. People look at me strangely when I tell them this.

The reason: I’ve started my cycling during winter in Seattle - yep, the same winter when we had 27 days straight of rain. When I explain my logic to people, they say its twisted. To me, its two simple things -

  • If I start cycling in winter & keep at it - I sure will enjoy cycling in summer
  • When its raining all the time, its easy to stay stuck indoors throughout the season - cycling is my way of getting out and feeling the wind in my hair
    What’s twisted about this, huh?! Now for my quibble - search is nowhere close to done (loosely related, Scoble saying the same about email search). Look at the route I cycle: though Virtual Earth has a fantastic map, there is no way for me to get a cycling route :(

This is not possible on Google, MSN or any other map search engine - I’ve to do the old school (sic!) and download and print out PDFs of the map route. My wish: that map search engines allow me to search for cycle routes.
