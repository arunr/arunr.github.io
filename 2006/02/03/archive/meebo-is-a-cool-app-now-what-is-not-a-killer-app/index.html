Meebo is a cool app, but its not a killer app

2006-02-03

I’ve been using Meebo for my IM for the last couple of days. Meebo is cool - it shows off the power of Ajax and shows how much the gap between client & web has narrowed.

However, it is not a killer app - its more proof of concept of new technology than a new idea: you find yourself thinking “This is neat… but why would I use this rather than my MSN messenger?!”.

The thing I’ve found with killer apps is: it takes a while to grok what the darn thing should be used for. [and this is why the network effect takes time to kick in and growth tends to be slow at the start for any killer app, leading to what is known as (incorrect imagery ahead) hockey stick growth]

So my measure of what a killer app is not - if I can grok it the first time I use it, its definitely not a killer app.
