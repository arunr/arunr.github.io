The cassette

2007-06-25

I’ve known Kanchuki for about 8 years now. I knew her first as one of my closest friends, and at that time I gave her a cassette of English songs - remeber, in 1998 it was a big deal to put together a custom album of songs, with no mp3’s or downloading of music.

In my mind, it is the classic collection of English songs for a guy to give a girl, an album that flows from one song to another - mushy, dance-y, reggae and mellow-rock. I was listening to the cassette today (it still is around, we’ve both heard it maybe 2000 times - most CD’s dont last that long!). Surprisingly, I dont think of any of the singers / bands below as my favorite - but the album came together well.

Here’s the play list, your thoughts are invited - any songs you would add / delete?

  1. Starts with “Annie’s song”, John Denver (OK, I know what you’re thinking - closest friend?! But it’s true…)
  2. Followed by “Unchained Melody”, Righteous Brothers (OK, shut up now…)
  3. Getting dance-y with the UB40 version of “Can’t help falling in love”
  4. Into the dance section - “La Bamba”, the Richie Valens version
  5. “That thing you do”, The Wonders
  6. “Walk of life”, Dire Straits
  7. “The bug”, Dire Straits
  8. Into reggae with “Buffalo soldier”, Bob Marley
  9. More reggae, “Two princes”, Spin Doctors
  10. Some more reggae, “Sweat”, Inner Circle
  11. “Baby I love your way”, the Big Mountain version
  12. A pop-funk number, “Come undone”, Duran Duran
  13. Ease into rock, “American Pie”, Don McLean - b.t.w, this song feels magical to me, this was the song playing in the background when I met Kanchuki.
  14. More rock, “Tequila sunrise”, The Eagles
  15. A mellow Dire Straits number, “Your latest trick”
  16. A re-mixed version of “Oh, pretty woman”, Ramom Y Mario
  17. Back to reggae, “Tease me”, Chaka Damus & Pliers
  18. Dance-y with a song that was hot in 1998, “Coco Jambo”, Mr. President (at least I didn’t put in any Vengaboys!)
  19. Rock, “Eye of the tiger”, Survivor (I had a few other songs in mind, I dont remember which now. However, I didn’t have any of those songs with me, so I settled for this song. It has grown on me since.)
  20. End with “Hysteria”, Def Leppard
